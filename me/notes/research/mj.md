
i want to maximize my marijuana yield. what organic methods do you suggest?

Maximizing cannabis yield organically comes down to getting a handful of fundamentals right. Here's what tends to make the biggest difference:

**Genetics and seed selection** Yield potential is largely set before you plant. Look for strains specifically bred for heavy yields (many indica-dominant and modern hybrids are). Feminized or clone-only cuts from a proven mother give you consistency.

**Living soil** This is probably the single biggest organic lever. Build a rich super-soil with quality base (peat or coco + aeration like perlite or rice hulls), compost or worm castings, and amendments like kelp meal, neem meal, crab meal, alfalfa meal, fish bone meal, gypsum, and basalt rock dust. Let it cook for 30+ days before planting. A healthy soil microbiome (mycorrhizae, beneficial bacteria) feeds the plant steadily and improves nutrient uptake.

**Compost teas and top-dressing** Aerated compost teas every couple of weeks deliver microbes and soluble nutrients. Top-dress with worm castings, kelp, and other amendments through the grow rather than relying on bottled nutrients.

**Light** Even organically grown plants are light-limited. Outdoors, pick the sunniest spot you have. Indoors, a quality full-spectrum LED at appropriate intensity (PPFD 600–900 in veg, 900–1200 in flower) makes a huge difference. Light is often the actual ceiling on yield.

**Training** Low-stress training (LST), topping, mainlining, and ScrOG (screen of green) flatten the canopy so more bud sites get direct light. This is free yield. Defoliating strategically during late veg and early flower opens airflow and light penetration.

**Environment** Aim for 75–82°F and 55–65% RH in veg, dropping to 40–50% RH in flower. Good airflow prevents mold and strengthens stems. CO2 helps but is hard to do organically in a sealed room.

**Watering and root zone** Water thoroughly but let the top inch dry between waterings. Fabric pots (5–15 gallon depending on plant size) air-prune roots and prevent overwatering. Healthy roots = bigger plants.

**Companion planting and IPM** Outdoors, plant clover, basil, marigolds, or yarrow to attract beneficials and suppress pests. Indoors, use predatory mites, ladybugs, or neem/karanja foliar sprays (stop foliars by week 3 of flower). Healthy plants resist pests far better than stressed ones.

**Timing the harvest** Don't harvest early. Watch trichomes with a loupe and pull when they're mostly cloudy with some amber for peak weight and potency.

A quick note: legality varies a lot by location, so make sure whatever you're doing is allowed where you live.

specifics of training? when, how tall?

Great question — training is where you can really unlock yield. Timing and technique matter a lot. Here's how I'd break it down:

**Topping** Top the plant after it has 4–6 nodes (usually 3–4 weeks from seed, or when it's 8–12 inches tall). Cut the main stem just above the 5th node. This forces two main colas instead of one. You can top again 1–2 weeks later once the new shoots have 3–4 nodes of their own, giving you 4 main colas. Some growers "mainline" by repeatedly topping to get 8 or 16 evenly spaced colas.

Stop topping at least 2 weeks before flipping to flower so the plant can recover.

**FIM (alternative to topping)** Instead of a clean cut, pinch off about 75% of the newest growth tip. It's less stressful and can produce 3–4 new colas from one cut. Good for sensitive strains.

**Low-Stress Training (LST)** Start as soon as the plant has 4–5 nodes and stems are flexible (around week 3). Gently bend the main stem sideways and tie it down with soft plant ties, pipe cleaners, or garden wire. The goal is a flat, even canopy. Keep bending new growth outward and downward so side branches catch up to the main cola height. LST continues through veg and into the first 2–3 weeks of flower (the "stretch").

**The rule of thumb:** keep the canopy flat and all bud sites within about 4–6 inches of the same height. This is where most indoor growers see the biggest yield bump.

**ScrOG (Screen of Green)** Install a trellis net 8–14 inches above your pot. As the plant grows into it, tuck branches horizontally under the squares to fill the screen evenly. Flip to flower when the screen is roughly 60–70% full. During the stretch, keep tucking until the canopy is full, then let the colas grow up through the net. ScrOG works beautifully with topping + LST combined.

**Defoliation** Two main windows: just before flipping to flower, and again around day 21 of flower (the "schwazzing" technique, though this is more aggressive). Remove fan leaves that block bud sites, large leaves in the lower canopy, and anything shading interior nodes. Don't overdo it — leave enough fans to power photosynthesis. A good guideline: you should still see mostly green when you step back.

**Lollipopping** 1–2 weeks into flower, strip the bottom third of the plant — small branches, popcorn buds, and leaves that won't get good light. This redirects energy to the top colas. Any bud site below the canopy line is fair game.

**Super cropping (advanced)** Gently pinch and bend a stem until the inner fibers crush but the outer skin stays intact. The stem heals with a knuckle and the branch can now be bent flat. Use this on stubborn tall branches during late veg or very early flower. It stresses the plant, so give it a few days to recover.

**Height targets**

- Top/FIM: 8–12 inches tall, 4–6 nodes
- Start LST: 6–10 inches, stems still flexible
- Flip to flower (indoor): 12–24 inches — plants typically double or triple in height during the stretch, so aim to finish at your light's sweet spot
- Final canopy: flat, with all colas within a few inches of each other

**Recovery** After any stress (topping, super cropping, heavy defoliation), give the plant 5–10 days to bounce back before doing more. Stacked stress events stall growth. Watch for drooping or yellowing — that's the plant telling you to back off.

**A simple weekly plan for photoperiod plants in veg:**

- Week 1–2: germination, seedling
- Week 3: top at 5th node, begin LST
- Week 4: continue LST, let recovery happen
- Week 5: top again (if mainlining), more LST
- Week 6–7: tuck into ScrOG net, light defoliation
- Week 8: final defoliation, flip to 12/12

Autoflowers are different — stick to LST only and do it early (week 2–3). They don't recover well from heavy stress like topping.
