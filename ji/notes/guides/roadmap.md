**Intersection Project Roadmap**  
  
## Phase 1: The Nerd AI (Research MVP)  
  
Goal: Prove that AI can help researchers connect fragmented facts and identify state crimes against democracy.  
  
Duration: 1 to 2 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Private, local LLM (DeepSeek) running on researcher laptops  
  
Curated library of 50 to 100 foundational documents (UN Charter, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Geneva Conventions, scholar texts)  
  
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) with clustered retrieval for reasoning across document types  
  
Simple chat interface for querying the library  
  
System prompt encoding the Prime Directive (Peace, Equality, Sustainability)  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
3 to 5 scholar contributors actively using the tool weekly  
  
Users report finding at least one novel connection they would have missed manually  
  
Qualitative feedback that the tool accelerates research  
  
Cost: Near zero (existing hardware, open source software)  
  
## Phase 2: The Scholar Collective (Private Research Platform)  
  
Goal: Expand the research tool into a collaborative environment where scholars share insights and build consensus.  
  
Duration: 2 to 4 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Shared, private cloud instance of the Context Engine (accessible to all contributors)  
  
Document upload and curation workflow for scholars  
  
Internal commenting and annotation system for discussing AI findings  
  
Basic notification system for new documents or flagged connections  
  
Exportable briefs (AI-generated summaries with citations)  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
10 to 20 active researcher contributors  
  
At least 500 curated documents in the library  
  
First consensus statements drafted on specific policy issues  
  
Researchers report the platform is essential to their workflow  
  
Estimated Cost: Low (cloud hosting for LLM, ~$200–$500/month)  
  
## Phase 3: The Public Newsletter (Civic MVP)  
  
Goal: Test the core public hypothesis: will users convert from passive readers to active stakeholders when journalism is paired with solutions and a solidarity button?  
  
Duration: 1 to 2 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Weekly email newsletter featuring one Problem Statement and one Solution Statement  
  
Simple "I Stand With This" button linked to a public tally  
  
Automated email to relevant targets (legislators, agencies) showing constituent support  
  
Issue-specific follow-up emails to track progress  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
1,000+ subscribers  
  
10%+ click-through rate on action button  
  
40%+ open rate sustained over 6 weeks  
  
Users report feeling a sense of agency  
  
Estimated Cost: Low (existing email platforms, ~$50–$100/month)  
  
## Phase 4: The Aggregator Prototype (Web Application)  
  
Goal: Build a lightweight web app that aggregates external journalism and adds the solidarity layer, replacing the manual newsletter.  
  
Duration: 3 to 5 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Web feed aggregating RSS feeds from partner journalists and independent outlets  
  
One-click solidarity pledge on each article  
  
User accounts to track individual pledge history  
  
Public leaderboards showing top issues and total solidarity counts  
  
Basic tagging of articles to Peace, Equality, or Sustainability tags  
  
Optional newsletter digest remains as an onboarding channel  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
5,000+ registered users  
  
5+ journalist partners contributing or linking content  
  
1,000+ solidarity pledges logged across issues  
  
Users return at least weekly  
  
Estimated Cost: Moderate (web development, cloud hosting, ~$2,000–$5,000 one-time + $500/month)  
  
## Phase 5: The Solution Engine (Pairing Problems with Solutions)  
  
Goal: Automate and scale the connection between journalism and vetted policy solutions.  
  
Duration: 3 to 4 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Solution database populated by partner issue groups (e.g., Debt Collective, [350.org](http://350.org/))  
  
AI-assisted matching: Context Engine suggests relevant solutions for each Problem Statement  
  
Solution pages with detailed policy proposals, supporting evidence, and action pathways  
  
User ability to propose new solutions or debate existing ones (moderated)  
  
Solution "endorsement" tally (users can back specific solutions, not just problems)  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
20+ vetted solution partners  
  
50% of articles have at least one matched solution  
  
Users engage with solution pages at 20%+ of article view rate  
  
Estimated Cost: Moderate (database development, partner outreach, ~$5,000–$10,000 one-time + $1,000/month)  
  
## Phase 6: The Functional Protest Engine (Quantified Strike Capability)  
  
Goal: Enable users to participate in decentralized, quantified protests with specific demands attached.  
  
Duration: 3 to 5 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Protest campaign creation tools (issue groups can define demands and action types: strike, boycott, divestment)  
  
User pledge to participate in specific actions (e.g., "I will not purchase from Company X until demand Y is met")  
  
Real-time tally of participants by location, action type, and demand  
  
Automated reporting to targets (e.g., "25,000 users in your district have pledged to withhold labor until Bill Z passes")  
  
Escalation thresholds (e.g., "At 50,000 pledges, we move from boycott to divestment campaign")  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
First successful quantified strike or boycott with measurable economic impact  
  
10,000+ users participate in at least one protest action  
  
Media coverage of the platform's unique protest mechanism  
  
Estimated Cost: Moderate to Significant (development of campaign tools, notification systems, ~$15,000–$25,000 one-time + $2,000/month)  
  
## Phase 7: The Personalized Feed (Social Media Scale)  
  
Goal: Build a full-scale social media style application where users engage daily with news as political events they can influence.  
  
Duration: 6 to 9 months  
  
Core Features:  
  
Personalized algorithmic feed prioritizing issues by user's solidarity history and systemic significance (civic priority over virality)  
  
User profiles showing pledge history, issue affiliations, and coalition memberships  
  
Following and discovery features (follow journalists, issue groups, or specific campaigns)  
  
Commenting and discussion threads on problem and solution statements  
  
Push notifications for protest calls, progress updates, and solution milestones  
  
Mobile apps (iOS and Android)  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
100,000+ registered users  
  
30%+ monthly active users  
  
10,000+ daily solidarity pledges  
  
First successful policy change directly attributable to platform-coordinated protest  
  
Estimated Cost: Significant (full stack development, mobile apps, scaling infrastructure, ~$100,000–$200,000 one-time + $10,000–$20,000/month)  
  
## Phase 8: The Virtuous Economy (Revenue & Sustainability)  
  
Goal: Transition from grant-funded project to self-sustaining institution through aligned revenue streams.  
  
Duration: Ongoing, begins at ## Phase 4 scale  
  
Core Features:  
  
Vetted advertising marketplace (only Peace, Equality, Sustainability aligned businesses)  
  
Local and small business promotion with sliding scale pricing  
  
Licensing of aggregated, anonymized solidarity data as high-fidelity public opinion research  
  
Member-owned collective structure with equitable revenue sharing for journalists and researchers  
  
Optional premium tier for advanced features (e.g., deeper analytics, early protest notifications)  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
Platform covers 75%+ of operating costs through earned revenue  
  
50+ aligned advertisers  
  
First data licensing agreement with an ethical research institution  
  
Journalist contributors report sustainable income from platform participation  
  
Estimated Cost: Revenue positive by design  
  
## Phase 9: The Autonomous Collective (Full Maturity)  
  
Goal: The platform operates as a self-governing, member-owned institution for democratic empowerment.  
  
Duration: Ongoing  
  
Core Features:  
  
Democratic governance structure (elected editorial and policy boards from member journalists and users)  
  
Open source codebase with community contributions  
  
Partnership network of issue groups, journalists, and researchers spanning multiple countries  
  
AI Context Engine continuously improved by scholar-curator community  
  
Platform serves as a model for democratic technology worldwide  
  
Success Metrics:  
  
1,000,000+ registered users  
  
100+ journalist partners  
  
200+ solution partners  
  
Documented policy changes achieved through platform-coordinated action  
  
Platform referenced in academic literature as a case study in digital democracy  
  
Estimated Cost: Sustained by virtuous economy model
