### **GROUP B** — tests that pin the old rules

- Many tests in the unit-test file use the old continuous degrees of freedom — a witness-length range as a degree of freedom and a slidable position as a continuous degree of freedom. The new spec replaces those with witness index (discrete) and label position. Locations: lines 69, 186, 298, and 343 through 450.
- Pair-separation tests around lines 112 to 136 pin the abandoned pair-check tier algorithm. The fifteen-pixel behaviour they enforce is still in the new spec (rule 5), but the test machinery is the wrong description.
- Silhouette-clearance tests around lines 434 to 486 use the combined convex hull of leaf parts as the reference description. The new spec uses the silhouette box. The fifteen-pixel behaviour survives but the reference description changed. These have salvage value — rewriting them to use the silhouette box keeps the rule-6 unit coverage rather than deleting it outright.
- The browser test that asserts every pair of drawn labels is at least thirty-three pixels apart conflicts with new rule 5 — the new rule says the gap is the silhouette margin, which is fifteen pixels per rule 8. Thirty-three is an old-design number. This test has salvage value too — change the threshold to fifteen and it tests new rule 5 directly.
- The greedy-seed, repair-pass, stochastic-finish, conflict-graph, and pair-check tier tests pin the abandoned avoidance algorithm. The new spec's placement algorithm (rule 19) picks an (edge, uniface) pair and then optimizes label position — not a greedy-plus-stochastic four-degree search.

## **PHASE 2. coding steps**, each runnable in the app at every step

The placement algorithm picks an (edge, uniface, witness index, label position) tuple per label, then optimizes the label position degree of freedom under the clearance constraints — one continuous degree of freedom, three discrete choices. **Evidence:** rules 1, 2, 3, and 19 of the uniface rules file.

Every phase 2 step has an exit criterion. Steps that only change internal data exit on "unit tests pass". Steps that change visible output exit on visual inspection — Jonathan reviews the rendered scene at four camera angles (front, three-quarter, top-down, tilted) saved at step 1.5 and reports accept or reject. On rejection: return to the step's design decision and either fix the code, switch to a listed alternative, or surface the rejection as a spec question to revisit before continuing.

Hard parts to expect bugs in: step 3a (four-degree-of-freedom search and rectangle-overlap math under perspective projection), step 3e (canvas font-metrics measurement for label widths), step 4a (rotated bounding-box construction), step 4b (overlap policy decision).     - **DONE step 3a — full four-degree-of-freedom search per rule 19.** A candidate is a possible (edge, uniface, witness index, label position) four-degree-of-freedom tuple — NOT the label itself. The label only exists once a candidate wins. For each part-axis, the search enumerates every candidate combination, runs each through the seven-filter pipeline (early exit on first failure), then scores the survivors.

        Hard filters (must pass; rules 5, 6, 14):
        1. Label rectangle clears the silhouette rect by at least fifteen pixels.
        2. Label rectangle clears every previously placed label rectangle by at least fifteen pixels.
        3. Label rectangle clears every previously placed witness anchor by at least fifteen pixels.
        4. The candidate's own two witness lines stay at least fifteen pixels apart along their length.
        5. **REMOVE:** Label rectangle clears every previously placed witness line by at least fifteen pixels.
        6. Label rectangle clears every previously placed dim line by at least fifteen pixels.
        7. The candidate's own anchors and dim line clear every previously placed label rectangle and every previously placed anchor by at least fifteen pixels.

        Slide repair runs when a position-sensitive filter fails (all but filter 4) — one slid candidate per (edge, uniface, witness index) re-enters the filter chain.

        Preferences (score the survivors; higher wins):
        1. Bonus when the label fits between the witness lines.
        2. Parabolic centering penalty along the dim line, peaking at twenty at the anchors.
        3. Penalty proportional to the screen-pixel length of the witnesses (shorter wins).
        4. Penalty proportional to the screen-pixel length of each witness inside the silhouette rect (witnesses must minimize passing through the design).
        5. STRONG penalty proportional to the world-coordinate perpendicular distance from the part to the uniface plane (closest uniface in 3D world coordinates wins by default; pixel-based terms only break ties).

        Witness index cascades through one, two, three. Real label widths flow from the dim-text formatter. Per-edge enumeration is in: four edges per axis (one at each corner pair of the bounding box). Eight pure helpers added (rectangle overlap, rectangle-to-rectangle distance, rectangle-inside-rectangle, rectangle-to-segment distance, closed-form segment-to-segment distance, point-to-segment distance, segments-intersect, bounding rectangle of a point set), all unit-tested. The records are then transposed: for every (direction, witness index) pair the running list of parts viable there. Per direction, the two witness indices with the longest lists win that direction. The part / witness-index pairs on those two winning lists are kept; every other (part, direction, witness index) viability record for that direction is discarded. A part viable in a direction only at a witness index that lost the vote loses that direction entirely and must place elsewhere (or drop, per rule 15). Open architectural question to settle before coding: the current loop checks each candidate against everything already placed by earlier parts in this render, so order matters. The vote needs to look across all parts at once, so the viability sweep must happen before any part commits. Two design options the implementer will choose between: (a) split the loop in two — a first sweep that judges every part in isolation against the silhouette and its own witnesses only, then the vote, then a commit pass that walks parts in order and applies the cross-part filters against the running placed-things state; or (b) keep the existing one-pass loop, tag each part's best result per (direction, witness index) as it goes, vote at the end, then revise each part's choice when its committed witness index lost the vote.
